1. Introduction

ARAPS (Academic Result Analysis and Prediction System) is a state-of-the-art academic web portal designed to monitor and improve student academic performance. By utilizing linear regression algorithms, ARAPS processes previous result data to generate predictive charts, subject-wise progression trends, and GPA trackers.

Target Users & Roles
  • Admin Panel Users: Superusers who configure accounts, groups, system structures, and view logs.
  • Teachers: Staff who manage individual marks, track attendance, analyze class cohorts, and import bulk result spreadsheets.
  • Students: Logged-in students viewing their marks history, letter grades, GPAs, attendance records, predictive outcomes, and trend indicators.
Live System URL

Access the portal live at: https://myresult-two.vercel.app

2. How to Login

Accessing the system requires valid credentials. Follow these steps to sign in:

Step-by-Step Instructions
1 Open your browser and navigate to the portal: https://myresult-two.vercel.app.
2 You will be greeted by the college landing page. Click the large blue Access Portal button.
3 On the Login form, enter your registered Username and Password exactly (case-sensitive).
4 Click the Sign In button to log in.

Role-Based Redirects

After clicking Sign In, the system automatically detects your role and forwards you to the corresponding panel:

  • Admin redirects directly to the backoffice administrator interface: /admin/.
  • Teacher redirects to the teacher marks panel: /teacher/marks/.

How to Logout

To sign out securely, locate the red Logout button in the navigation bar at the top-right of your panel. Click it to terminate your session and return to the login interface.

3. Admin Guide

Administrators manage the system configuration, view transaction logs, and manage student accounts.

3.1 Accessing the Admin Panel

Logged-in administrators can access the backoffice panel directly by appending /admin/ to the domain name: https://myresult-two.vercel.app/admin/.

3.2 Adding a Student
1 Navigate to Users (under Authentication) → click Add User. Set username/password.
2 Go to Students (under Students app) → click Add Student. Link the newly created User account, enter their full name, roll number, and class name (e.g. BSCS-7A). Click Save.
3.3 Adding a Teacher
1 Navigate to Users → click Add User. Input username and password.
2 Under the user's details, scroll to the Groups section. Assign them to the Teacher group by moving it to the "Chosen Groups" column.
3 Click Save at the bottom of the page.
3.4 Viewing Audit Logs

Audit logs track critical events (e.g. when attendance is taken). Navigate to Audit Logs (under Results app) to view details, timestamps, and usernames involved in system changes.

3.5 Viewing All Results and Attendance

Under the Admin Panel, select Results or Attendances to view, modify, or delete any record in bulk across all students and classes.

Admin Bulk Import Shortcut

A top-menu shortcut titled Bulk Import Students is available on the administrator navigation header. You can click this to upload student listings directly without going through multiple Django forms.

4. Teacher Guide

Teachers enter student results, track attendance, and analyze class performance.

4.1 Adding Student Marks

Navigate to the **Marks Management** dashboard and find the "Add New Result" card at the top:

1 Select a Student from the dropdown.
2 Select a Subject.
3 Input Marks (between 0.0 and 100.0).
4 Input the Term (e.g., Mid, Final) and Year (e.g., 2025).
5 Click Add to commit the record.
4.2 Editing/Deleting Marks

Scroll down to the All Results table on the dashboard. Use the yellow Edit button to modify results or the red Delete button to remove a record. Deleting a record requires confirming the deletion on a confirmation screen showing the grade detail.

4.3 Marking Attendance
1 Click Attendance in the top navigation bar.
2 Select the target Date. (Defaults to today's date).
3 For each student, check Present or Absent.
4 Click the blue Submit button to record attendance.
4.4 Class Performance Analytics & Chart

The **Class Performance Analytics** section provides instant cohort calculations:

  • Filters: Select a Subject, Term, and Year to load records. The page auto-submits and displays analytics.
  • Chart.js Bar Chart: Automatically draws a colored bar representing marks for each student. Bars are color-coded: Green (marks ≥ 80%), Blue (marks ≥ 60%), and Red (marks < 60%).
  • Performer Ranks: Evaluates overall student averages and ranks the **Top 3** and **Bottom 3 (Need Attention)** students automatically.
4.5 Bulk Import Results from CSV/Excel
1 Click the Bulk Import Results (CSV/Excel) button under the Class Analytics section.
2 Click Download Sample CSV Template to get the correct spreadsheet format.
3 Open the template in Excel or Google Sheets, add student marks (using roll numbers or usernames), and save the file.
4 Click **Choose File**, select your spreadsheet, and click **Upload and Import**.
Atomic Validation Rule

The bulk import engine is atomic. If a single row is incorrect (e.g. invalid roll number, marks out of range), the entire import is aborted. Correct all errors printed on the page and re-upload.

5. Student Guide

Students can review their academic progress, check prediction metrics, and download results reports.

5.1 Student Dashboard Overview

Upon log in, the Student Dashboard renders cards displaying summary metrics:

  • Overall GPA: The average grade points calculated across all results on a 4.0 scale.
  • Term GPA: Calculated specifically for your latest enrolled semester (e.g., Final 2024).
  • Attendance Percentage: Total present days divided by total tracked calendar days.
5.2 Understanding Predictions & Grades
  • Letter Grade: Next to each subject, you will see your grade (e.g. A, B+, B, C, F) calculated dynamically.
  • Predicted Next Marks: ARAPS generates a predicted percentage score for your next term. This indicates your expected outcome if you maintain your current learning rate. If you only have one result, it will safely show "N/A" (as at least two results are needed for regression).
5.3 Subject Performance Trends

Your performance trends are evaluated using linear regression lines and displayed as color-coded indicators:

  • Improving: Marks trend upward across terms.
  • Stable: Marks show minor natural fluctuations.
  • Declining: Marks trend downward across terms.
5.4 Downloading/Printing Results Report

To print or download your report card as a PDF:

1 Click the **HTML Report** button on the student dashboard.
2 Press Ctrl + P (Command + P on Mac) on your keyboard to open the browser print dialog.
3 Select **Save as PDF** as the destination printer. Choose layout orientation and click **Save**.
5.5 Viewing Notifications

Locate the **Bell icon** in your dashboard header. The number count shows unread messages. Click it to view details on newly uploaded marks or announcements.
